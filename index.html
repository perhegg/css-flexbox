La Scala, in full Teatro alla Scala (Italian: “Theatre at the Stairway”), theatre in Milan, one of the principal opera houses of the world and the leading Italian house.

Built in 1776–78 by Empress Maria Theresa of Austria (whose country then ruled Milan), it replaced an earlier theatre that had burned. In 1872 it became the property of the city of Milan.
